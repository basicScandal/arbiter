Judge Scoring Reference Card

Criteria + Track Bonus

Each criterion is scored 0–10. The final score is the weighted average of general criteria scores plus any applicable track bonus. The AI judge score is blended 70/30 with the human judge score.

Calibration Anchors

Range Calibration
9 – 10 Exceptional — Among the best you've seen. Sets a new standard.
7 – 8 Strong — Clearly above average, notable quality with minor gaps.
5 – 6 Adequate — Meets baseline expectations, nothing remarkable.
3 – 4 Below Average — Significant shortcomings, partially delivered.
1 – 2 Poor — Fundamental failures, barely attempted or broken.

General Criteria

Technical Execution 40%

Implementation quality, functionality, complexity, code correctness, edge case handling

9 – 10 Flawless implementation, production-quality, handles edge cases
7 – 8 Solid implementation with minor gaps
5 – 6 Functional but rough, obvious shortcuts
3 – 4 Partially working, significant bugs
1 – 2 Barely functional or broken

Innovation 30%

AI x Security novelty, creative approaches, original thinking

9 – 10 Groundbreaking novel approach
7 – 8 Clearly innovative with unique angle
5 – 6 Some novelty but mostly established techniques
3 – 4 Incremental or derivative
1 – 2 No discernible innovation

Demo Quality 30%

Clear explanation, working live demo, effective presentation, compelling narrative

9 – 10 Flawless live demo, masterful explanation, compelling narrative
7 – 8 Solid demo with good explanation, minor hiccups
5 – 6 Demo works but explanation unclear or rushed
3 – 4 Demo partially works, confusing presentation
1 – 2 Demo fails or no meaningful demonstration

Note: Extended Criteria Variant

4-Criterion Variant: An extended rubric may be used matching the nebulafog.ai website, splitting into Technical Execution (30%), Innovation (25%), Impact (20%), and Demo Quality (25%). The AI judge's ScoringEngine accepts an optional criteria parameter to switch between variants. The calibration anchors and track bonuses remain unchanged.
