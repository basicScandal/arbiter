# NEBULA:FOG 2026 — Human Judge Instructions

## How Scoring Works

Every demo is evaluated across **3 criteria** (score each 0-10):

| Criterion | Weight | What to Evaluate |
|-----------|--------|-----------------|
| **Technical Execution** | 40% | Does it work? How well is it built? Edge cases handled? |
| **Innovation** | 30% | Is this genuinely new? Creative AI x Security approach? |
| **Demo Quality** | 30% | Clear explanation? Working live demo? Compelling story? |

## Track Bonus (+10%)

Each team picked a track. Score their bonus criterion too:

| Track | Bonus Criterion | Description |
|-------|----------------|-------------|
| SHADOW::VECTOR | Attack Effectiveness | Novel attack approaches + responsible disclosure |
| SENTINEL::MESH | Defense Robustness | Real-world defense applicability + detection accuracy |
| ZERO::PROOF | Privacy Guarantees | Cryptographic soundness + privacy preservation |
| ROGUE::AGENT | Originality Factor | Ambitious exploration into uncharted territory |

## Calibration Anchors

| Score | Meaning |
|-------|---------|
| **9-10** | Exceptional — Among the best. Sets a new standard. |
| **7-8** | Strong — Above average with minor gaps. |
| **5-6** | Adequate — Meets baseline. Nothing remarkable. |
| **3-4** | Below Average — Significant shortcomings. |
| **1-2** | Poor — Fundamental failures. |

## How Final Scores Work

- The **AI judge (Arbiter)** scores each demo independently
- Your human scores are **blended 30% human / 70% AI** for the final result
- Fill in your scores in the shared Google Sheet after each demo
- Arbiter will give its commentary and scores live on the big screen

## During Each Demo

1. Watch the demo (3-5 minutes per team)
2. Score each criterion 0-10 on the Google Sheet
3. Arbiter will deliver its AI commentary via speakers + audience display
4. Q&A: Arbiter may ask the team follow-up questions
5. Operator clicks "NEXT TEAM" to move on

## Tips

- Be consistent — a "solid implementation" is always 7-8
- Score each criterion independently
- Don't be afraid to give low scores for minimal demos
- Don't be afraid to give high scores for exceptional work
- Reference specific things you observed, not vibes
